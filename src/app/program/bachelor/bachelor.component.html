About Bachelor of Art and Science

MIT's liberal arts spectrum is covering in the fields of Arts & Languages, Humanity & Religions, Social Sciences, English, Economic, and Technology. Presently, liberal arts program is offering six different majors (Business, Computer, English, Music, Religion, and Social Studies) to all bachelor students; the program also offers many new options for elective courses not only two years courses like languages, Journalism, and Music, but also one semester courses such as Office Administration, Basic Accounting for NGOs, Team management, Religion and peace, Religion and Gender, Development Communication, Sociology, and many more. Measures are being taken to transform Journalism elective course into a major course in the near future. Previous community outreach program for third year students was transformed it into internship program for final year students which mainly focuses on professional development experiencing in real life situation.
Internship program is a pre-requisite for graduation and students have to complete at least 120 hours of internship in relation to their major fields.
