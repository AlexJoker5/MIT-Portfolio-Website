Social Studies Major

Department will empower the students to create their own destiny for seeking justice and common good through a process of social transformation.
On successful completion of the social studies students will be able to:
  • Integrate and value the importance of social studies
  • Engage on social, political and economic related issues critically.
  • Initiate for the common good of society.

The Following Subjects are Available in Social Studies Major

First Year (Compulsory)

01 : Civic Education

The course aspires to provide an overview of some of the most important ideas related to active citizenship, reflecting on the civic knowledge, civic skills and civic values that are fundamental required for every individual to become active citizens. This module is specially designed to inspire Myanmar students in reaching to the full extent of their participation in the community. It endeavors to bring about an empowerment among knowledgeable citizens who will act as change agents responsible for establishing social justice and preventing human rights abuses.

Second Year of Computer Studies

01 : Human Rights (Compulsory)

This course has been specifically designed to address the history and issue of Democracy and Human Rights. The course begins by examining the basic of the international human rights movement. Second, students become acquainted with the methodology of human rights fact-finding, with attention given to learning and practicing interview technique. Third, the course challenges students to think as human rights advocates in their examination of specific foreign policy choices and NGO strategies designed to advance human rights. Including the use of military force to promote human rights, the development of international criminal courts, truth commissions, and other attempts at transitional justice, the intersection of humanitarian and human rights law, with an introduction to war crimes, crimes against humanity and the specific crime of genocide, advocacy strategies concerning globalization and transnational corporations, and the human rights dimensions of terrorism.

02 : Introduction to Social Studies

This course is the combined study of human society within four main subjects: history, geography, economics and politics. It explores the interactions between different peoples and many fundamental aspects of the daily life.

03 : Introduction to Anthropology

This course let the students to look at cross-cultural differences in social institution, cultural beliefs, and communication styles. An anthropology class can be useful for students in variety of majors, such education, business, medicine, and law. Knowledge about human diversity is helpful in many careers. It is often an advantage to have developed an approach for learning about interaction with people who are different.

04 : Modern History

Modern history is one of the most crucial and major subject of Social Science. Every decision, movement, revolution, founding of new state, instead of saying, civilization, societies, States shaped nowadays human history. Reversely, history also shaped the world that we live today. Changes of every single process of history can be learned by this course.

05 : Economic Analysis

This course is designed and introduction to the economic principles. Economic analysis provides a basic understanding of the Global economic system to enhance with policies, and the planning and implementation. It also brings about the amelioration of economically backward communities. And also give to the study of the socio cultural factors which influence economic activity and development.

Third Year of Social Studies

01 : Environmental Policy and Politics

This course aims to provide the key aspects of the management of people and institutions in relation to environmental change and response and the rise of the environment as policy and a political issue. The course opens the space for students to have critical discussion on the range of ethics and ideological positioning towards the environment with the case study at local, national and global level. As an overall, the course will examine the understanding of "Sustainable development" for the country context.

02 : Community Work

This course is about the relevant social change and who will be able to intervene in social problems related to the functioning of community development. Community work, primarily designed for applied social science students who would now have the professional knowledge of the method; but who might have been introduced to the process. Approach through non-formal trainings and, or actual work with communities.

03 : Sociology

The discipline of sociology has frequently been characterized as the "science of industrial society". While the discipline's founding fathers; Emile Durkheim, Karl Marx and Max Weber addressed the origins and consequences of industrial growth in nineteenth century Europe, we will examine a wide array of social problems in a host of different historical and geographic context.

04 : International Relation

Students will understand more about Theories of International Relations and International Politics. The course will explain about Realism, Idealism, Constructivism, Gender, Globalization, Marxism, Modernization theories of International Relations. There will be case studies of International Politics based on these theories.

05 : Political Geography

This course will give knowledge about how political movements and decisions affect the locations and borders and how the geographic locations affect politics. They will also get knowledge about the relationship among politics and geography, demography and economics, especially with respect to the foreign policy of a nation and a combination of geographic and political factors relating to or influencing a nation or region.

Fourth Year of Social Studies

01 : Critical Thinking for Social Studies

The goal of this course is to help students identify, evaluate, and construct inductive and deductive arguments in spoken and written forms. In general, students will be encouraged to develop the habits of assessing and defending the reasonableness of their beliefs and values; appreciate the importance to looking at an issue from a variety of points of view and of recognizing the complexity that surrounds most controversial issues.

02 : Social Psychology

In order to facilitate human growth, the social science practitioner must have an understanding of the person as a social being. Through understanding of research in social psychology, personal awareness of relationships and application of Biblical perspectives on relationships, this unit will enable the student to develop knowledge of the person as an individual and as a member of society. Greater awareness of the person as a social being will assist the students to more fully understand both interpersonal and intrapersonal behavior and attitudes, to relate more fully and maturely to others, and to contribute positively in the development of society. Understanding of the behavior and attitudes within and between groups contributes to growth within societal structures, as well as positive development of organizations and social groups. This course will also help students to get in a deeper understanding in social psychology with the application for any Christian desiring to contribute to a just society, as well as to the social science practitioner in people-helping relationships.

03 : Public Policy

This purpose of this course is to introduce students to basic principles and key concepts of public policy. It also familiarize students definitions and basic ideas of different focal actors and institution and a view of how the processes of public policy operate from agenda setting through formulation and legitimating, to implementation and eventual evaluation with examples of case studies using a number of analytical approaches to critically discuss each process. In addition, it examines major policy issues confronting contemporary society, and participation of civil society organizations and public in political and social change.

04 : Development Perspectives and Processes

This course aims to introduce the different views concerning the definition and process of development which will be providing the students with basic knowledge, attitudes and skills in development, as a strategy for social change. It also serves as a venue for the students to examine and analyze various frameworks and strategies in working with people, in relation to their present and future involvement toward sustainability the nation's task of building and its move for national development through the practice of democracy.

05 : Strategic Environmental Assessment

This course develops a sympathetic understanding on what are the various assessments for environmental issues, what the processes are and how to use them in practical way. The students will be engendered key capacities, techniques and skills for analyzing by identifying the role, positioning and strategies of the key factors involved in different case studies. The tools of political ecology will be the center of discussion to encompass economic growth, environmental protection and social justice.

06 : Political Thoughts

This course is an introductory investigation of selected contemporary political thoughts and their relations to the struggles for freedom and democracy of people around the world. Common themes of the course include governance, individual liberty, justice and responsibility for the collective wellbeing of society. By studying the evolving constructs of contemporary political thoughts, the students will gain insight in different approaches that leaders use to solve complex problems of governance and maintenance of social order.
