Religion Major

Religion Department in Liberal Arts Program is coordinated by theological school of Myanmar Institute of Theology. Course in Religious Department are instructed by faculty of theological school. The theological school formed Committee to oversee the Religious Department. Religion Major offers the following subjects

The Following Subjects are Available in Religion Major

First Year (Compulsory)

01 : World Religious

This course is designed to introduce beliefs and practices of world religious. This course will cover the origins and development of beliefs and practice of Primal Religious, Hinduism, Judaism, Islam, Confucianism, Shintoism, Taoism, Sikhism and Jainism.

02 : Introduction to Christianity

This course will examine the nature and development Christianity. Attention will be given to the central teachings of Christianity with reference to their impact upon Christians' lives and experiences throughout the history.

Second Year of Religion

01 : Religion and Society (Compulsory)

This course is designed to study the interplay between religion and society. This course will cover how religion has great impact in shaping communal life. This course will deal with the issue of the question of the relationship between religion and conflicts, religion and power.

02 : Hebrew Bible & Ancient Israel

This course is the study of the beginning of Ancient Israel writings and their theological and historical development. This course is also studying the remarkable religious and social movements as well as nature and basic message of Hebrew Bible (Old Testament).

03 : History of Christianity

This course is a study of Christian History from its beginning to the reformation. The course highlights the important events or issues that shaped the movements, thoughts, practices and faith of the church. It is not merely a study of the historical facts but an analytical and interpretive study of the facts followed by a recommendation of what the present church should do or should be.

04 : Theravada Buddhism

This course introduces the history and development of Theravada Buddhism in India, Siri Lanka and Myanmar. While the history and development of Theravada Buddhism is the focus of the study, major themes and teachings in Theravada Buddhism will be highlighted.

05 : Gandhi and his legacy

This course is designed to introduce the life and thought of Gandhi, His understanding of religious faith in daily life and the role of religious teachings in daily life, particularly political life will be considered.

Third Year of Religion

01 : Ethics (Compulsory)

This course is to introduce the nature of moral reasoning. The course will deal with the question of what makes a person or a community to have good life and good character. The ethical problems and dilemma of concrete issue will be deal with in reference to the question of what makes good life and good character.

02 : Reading The New Testament

Reading the New Testament is a course to introduce beliefs and practices of Early Christianity in its historical settings. The origin, purpose, content and theme of the New Testament will be examined in their historical context.

03 : Religion and Gender

This course is designed to introduce that religion is the experience of both men and women. This course will explore religion is both the experience of men and women, and religion symbols point men and women beyond their ordinary life.

04 : Religion & Peace

This course is designed to deal with the ideal of religion, peace and their interactions. How religion plays the role in conflict, violence and peace building are clarified through different religious studies and case studies, which inform the potentials to peace.

05 : Christian Philosophy & Theology

This course is designed to study the beliefs and practices of Christianity with particular attention to Modern Christian thinkers. The course will examine Christian understanding of God, Human Being, Salvation, Eschatology, etc.

05 : Practices of Buddhist Meditation

This course explores the theories and practices of meditation developed by the major Buddhist traditions. The main focus is on the Theravada school and on the meditative traditions of other school.

Fourth Year of Religion

01 : Contemporary Christian Thought

This course is designed to introduce life and thought of 20th Century Christian thinkers such as: Karl Barth, Paul Tillich, Dietrich Bonhoeffer, JurgenMoltmann and etc.

02 : Inter-religions Dialogue

This course is designed to introduced the students how the importance of Interreligious Dialogue in our pluralistic society and in the global age.

03 : Religion & Spirituality

This course is to introduce traditions of spirituality in various religious faiths; attention will be given to Buddhist Spirituality and Christian Spirituality in a dialogical way.

04 : Christianity Today

This course will introduce Christian movements in Contemporary world. The nature of the course is to study trends of Global Christianity and will give attention particularly to Christianity in Myanmar. A study on Myanmar Christian thinkers will be made.

05 : Buddhism Today

This course will introduce Buddhists movement in Contemporary world. The nature of the course is to study trends of Global Buddhism and will give attention particularly to Popular Buddhism in Myanmar. A study on Myanmar Buddhists thinker will be made.

06 : Islam Today

This course will introduce Muslims movement in Contemporary world. The nature of the course is to study trends of Global Islamic Community and will give attention particularly to Islam in Myanmar. A study on Myanmar Muslim thinkers will be made.
